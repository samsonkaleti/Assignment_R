Top 10 Rivers in India

  1. Ganges

    The Ganges is one of the major rivers in India, flowing through the northern part of the country. It is considered holy by Hindus and is used for various religious rituals and ceremonies.

  2. Yamuna

    The Yamuna is a major tributary of the Ganges and flows through several states in northern India. It is considered sacred in Hinduism and is associated with the goddess Yamuna.

  3. Brahmaputra

    The Brahmaputra is one of the major rivers in India and flows through northeastern India and Bangladesh. It is known for its immense volume of water and is an important river for irrigation and transportation.

  4. Indus

    The Indus is one of the longest rivers in Asia, flowing through India, Pakistan, and China. It has great historical and cultural significance, being the birthplace of the Indus Valley Civilization.

  5. Narmada

    The Narmada is a major river in central India, known for its role in the cultural and religious heritage of the region. It is often referred to as the lifeline of Madhya Pradesh.

  6. Krishna

    The Krishna is one of the longest rivers in India, flowing through the states of Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh. It is known for its importance in agriculture and hydroelectric power generation.

  7. Godavari

    The Godavari is one of the major rivers in India, flowing through the states of Maharashtra, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, and Odisha. It is often referred to as the Dakshin Ganga.

  8. Mahanadi

    The Mahanadi is a major river in eastern India, flowing through the states of Chhattisgarh and Odisha. It is known for its fertile delta region and is an important source of water for irrigation.

  9. Kaveri

    The Kaveri is one of the major rivers in southern India, flowing through the states of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. It is known for its cultural and historical significance and is often referred to as the Ganga of the South.

  10. Tapti

    The Tapti is a river in central India, flowing through the states of Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Gujarat. It is known for its role in the agricultural economy of the region.
