Top 10 Temples in India

  1. Venkateswara Temple, Tirumala

    Located in Andhra Pradesh, this temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, a form of the Hindu god Vishnu. It is one of the richest and most visited religious sites in the world.

  2. Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Varanasi

    Situated in the holy city of Varanasi, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the most sacred Hindu temples and a major pilgrimage site.

  3. Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), Amritsar

    The holiest shrine in Sikhism, the Golden Temple is known for its stunning architecture and its sacred pool ("Sarovar"), which is said to have healing powers.

  4. Meenakshi Amman Temple, Madurai

    This historic temple in Tamil Nadu is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (a form of Parvati) and her consort Lord Sundareswarar (a form of Shiva). It is renowned for its magnificent architecture and intricate carvings.

  5. Jagannath Temple, Puri

    Located in Odisha, this temple is dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Vishnu), along with his siblings Balabhadra and Subhadra. The annual Rath Yatra (chariot festival) at this temple is famous worldwide.

  6. Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, Srirangam

    This temple complex in Tamil Nadu is dedicated to Lord Ranganatha (a reclining form of Vishnu). It is one of the largest temples in India and a significant pilgrimage destination.

  7. Kedarnath Temple, Uttarakhand

    Situated in the Himalayas, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is considered one of the holiest Hindu temples.

  8. Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur

    Also known as the Big Temple, this UNESCO World Heritage Site in Tamil Nadu is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is famous for its architectural grandeur, particularly its towering vimana (temple tower).

  9. Siddhivinayak Temple, Mumbai

    Dedicated to Lord Ganesha, this temple in Mumbai is one of the most visited temples in the city. It is believed to fulfill the wishes of its devotees.

  10. Vaishno Devi Temple, Jammu and Kashmir

    Located in the Trikuta Mountains, this temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Vaishno Devi. Pilgrims trek uphill to reach the temple, which is a symbol of faith and devotion.
